About

I'm an undergraduate senior at the University of Colorado Boulder.
My major is in Ecology & Evolutionary Biology; however, I have taken
double as many courses in Applied Math (probability & statistics) and
Geography (remote sensing).

I began my research experience in bioinformatics, but I found a
paid job at the National Snow and Ice Data Center, creating and
documenting remotely sensed data products. With an analysis and
remote sensing background, I landed an Analytics Hub internship at
CU/CIRES's Earth Lab.

For the last couple years, I have made predictive models for the
occurance, management, and impacts of natural disasters. These
projects have focused on tornadoes and wildfires and have required
a broad set of technical skills, including but not limited to:
  • Deep Learning
  • AWS EC2, S3
  • Docker
  • CPU/GPU parallelization
  • Python, Bash, R
